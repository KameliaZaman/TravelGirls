CHINA

The world's oldest civilization, with a dazzing modern face

Travelling to China may seem intimidating to solo female travellers, but the country offers rich cultural experiences and in short, is very safe. China has low crime rates, and violent crimes against tourists are particulalry rare due to harsh criminal penalities. However, you should still execrise caution in busy tourist areas as pickpocketing is still common. For female travellers, catcalling is extremely rare and considered very crude in China due to cultural norms, so you should feel comfortable in the streets alone. Hopping between Chinas major cities could not be easier or safer thanks to super-clean and safe high speed bullet trains. The country hosts impressive natural wonders, facinating antient heritage and unique cuisine wich should not be missed.

★ Our Top Tips ★
  1. Always remember to carry tissues as many public toilets to do provide toilet roll. Also, be prepared to use squat toilets in most public places
  2. Public transport in Chinese cities is the most popular way to travel. It is highly subsdised by the government so perfect for budget concious travellers. Make sure you are prepared with small change if you plan to use public buses.
  3. If you are looking for someone who speaks English, high school aged children will be likely to have the best knowlegde.
  4. Access to social media such as Facebook, Instagram and Youtube is blocked in the country. If you want to keep in touch with home or share your travel snaps you will need to download of VPN. Find more information about VPNs here.
