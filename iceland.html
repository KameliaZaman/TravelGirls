ICELAND

The safest destination on earth

First on our list is the land of ice and fire, Iceland, a unique and beautiful country perfect for first time solo women travellers. According to the Global Peace Index, Iceland is the safest country in the country and has been for the last 12 years, with low crime rates and a ‘small-town’ feeling, even in the capital city. The country is also extremely progressive in terms of gender equality and attitudes towards sexuality. Due to its proximity to the UK and mainland Europe, Iceland can easily be built into a Euro-trip and is ideal for shorter weekend break. Iceland can be expensive, but budget hostels and affordable Airbnb’s are available in residential areas.

★ Our Top Tips ★
  1. Don’t forget to pack warm clothes, waterproofs and hiking gear- Iceland is cool year round with highs of about 15 C in summer and it can get very cold at night.
  2. Hiring a car is a great option when travelling in Iceland but not feasible for everyone. Luckily there are lots of all inclusive day trips available which take you from Reykjavik to some off the countries top attractions.
  3. Very few banks outside of Iceland carry Icelandic Krona. Make sure you change your money before you go to the airport at the end of your holiday to avoid being stuck with any unusable Krona.
  4. Be aware that in the winter, daylight hours are very short and it is dark very early- solo female travellers may want to avoid the darker months.
